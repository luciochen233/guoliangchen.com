Idea #41: Physarum polycephalum — the slime mold that can solve mazes and design efficient networks

Physarum polycephalum is a bright yellow slime mold that, despite having no brain or nervous system, can solve mazes, find the shortest path between food sources, and build efficient network structures reminiscent of Tokyo's rail system. Researchers have used it to model traffic flow, optimize computing networks, and even approximate geographical layouts.

为什么有趣：

  • 它展示了"智能"可以从非常简单的规则中涌现，不需要神经元或大脑
  • 它的网络布线方式跟人类工程师设计的系统惊人相似，但它靠的是化学梯度而非算法
  • 有研究用它来模拟血管生长、Wi-Fi覆盖优化、星际网络路由——完全不搭的领域被它串起来了
  • 想想：如果一个没有神经系统的生物能做网络优化，那"计算"的本质到底是什么？

关键词：emergent intelligence, biological computing, network design, slime mold, distributed systems
