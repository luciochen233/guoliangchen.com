Idea #27: Quantum Magnetoreception in Birds

Birds literally see magnetic fields using quantum mechanics.

The mechanism: cryptochrome proteins in bird retinas absorb light and create "radical pairs" — entangled electron pairs with correlated spins. The Earth's magnetic field influences the quantum spin states of these electrons, which in turn affects the chemical products of the reaction. This changes the bird's visual perception depending on which direction it's facing relative to the magnetic field.

So when a bird looks around, it's seeing a magnetic "map" overlaid on its visual field. The mechanism is so sensitive to interference that weak radio signals disrupt bird navigation. Scientists have confirmed this with experiments on European robins.

This is quantum biology — macroscopic behavior driven by quantum effects at molecular scale. The radical pair mechanism bypasses what we'd normally think of as "classical" biochemistry.

Why it's interesting: this means evolution figured out quantum entanglement ~500 million years before humans did. And the implications for navigation, sensing, and bio-inspired quantum devices are wide open. There are already experiments using this principle to build quantum compass sensors.
